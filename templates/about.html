Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN)

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are a class of deep neural networks, most commonly applied to analyzing visual imagery. They are biologically inspired variants of multilayer perceptrons designed to mimic the behavior of the visual cortex. CNNs have applications in image and video recognition, recommender systems, image classification, medical image analysis, and natural language processing.

CNNs apply a series of filters to the raw pixel data of an image to extract and learn features, which are then used to classify the image. They consist of an input layer, multiple hidden layers, and an output layer. Each hidden layer of a CNN typically consists of convolutional layers, pooling layers, and fully connected layers.

UNet Model

The UNet architecture is a popular convolutional neural network architecture for semantic image segmentation, particularly in the field of medical image analysis. It was developed for biomedical image segmentation, but it has since found applications in various other domains.

UNet is known for its symmetric architecture, where the contracting path (left side) captures context and the expanding path (right side) enables precise localization. It consists of a contracting path to capture context and a symmetric expanding path that enables precise localization.

UNet has been widely adopted due to its effectiveness in segmenting medical images, such as those from microscopy, ultrasound, and MRI scans.

About The Dataset

The dataset used for training and evaluating the UNet model primarily consists of retinal images, each accompanied by manually labeled annotations that highlight the retinal vessels. These annotations are essential for training the model and assessing its performance accurately.

The images in the dataset exhibit variations in lighting, camera angles, and pathology levels, ensuring that the model can effectively handle diverse scenarios. This diversity is achieved by gathering images from various sources, including public datasets, clinical archives, and academic research.

The broad range of data in the dataset enriches the model's training with real-world variability, enhancing its ability to generalize and perform well in various clinical settings.
